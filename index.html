About This Gallery

The Pokémon Trading Card Game has been home to extraordinary artwork since 1996. This gallery celebrates the artists who bring Pokémon to life on cardboard — from the iconic original Base Set illustrators to modern digital artists.

Card data and images are sourced in real-time from the Pokémon TCG API, a community-maintained database of all Pokémon TCG cards.

Features

  • Browse by Artist — Click any artist card to see every card they've illustrated, pulled live from the TCG API.
  • Panoramic Scene Sets — Curated sets of cards whose artwork connects into a larger illustration. Use the Assemble button to see them merge.
  • Card Lightbox — Click any card for a full-size view. Use arrow keys or buttons to navigate between cards.
  • Search — Filter artists by name, tags, or description using the search bar.

Notable Pokémon TCG Artists

The TCG has featured hundreds of artists over its 28+ year history. Some of the most celebrated include Mitsuhiro Arita (the original Charizard), Yuka Morii (clay sculptures), 5ban Graphics (sleek EX/GX full arts), and Tomokazu Komiya (beloved for cozy storybook scenes).

About Scene Sets

Multi-card panoramas have been a beloved feature of the Pokémon TCG since the early sets. Illustrators design adjacent cards so that when placed side by side, their backgrounds merge into a single continuous landscape — a reward for collectors who hunt down complete sets.

Notable examples include the Cosmic Eclipse trainer full-art celebration scene, the Pokémon 151 ghost-type evolutionary trio, and the Evolving Skies Dragon-type sky panorama.
